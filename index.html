Why It Maps to Business Revenue

Chime earns interchange revenue every time a member swipes their debit card. Referrals are one of Chime's most efficient acquisition channels. When a referred member sets up direct deposit, Chime becomes their primary bank, meaning more frequent transactions, higher spend volume, and longer retention. Every percentage point lift in C2 DD CVR translates directly to more active members generating interchange revenue. CAC efficiency means Chime acquires high-value members through referrals without eroding the margin those members produce. Card activation within 14 days is the final gate that confirms a referred member is truly active and eligible for their incentive. This is the moment a referral becomes a revenue-generating Chime customer.

VEGA: Variable Evergreen General Audience

From failed ML model to personalized offer engine. Proven in promos, then scaled to evergreen. Originally built for C1 referrers, now being tested for C2 referred members.

1

The Hypothesis

Members convert better when the offer reflects their predicted likelihood, not a flat $100 for everyone.

2

First Attempt: DSML Optimization

ML-powered targeting yielded neutral lift after adjusting for sample imbalance. Diagnosed the failure and pivoted fast.

3

The Pivot: Randomized Offer Testing

Shifted to randomized offer amounts ($100, $125, $150, $200) across 7M+ members. Proper holdout via ExPlat. 50/50 split. 11-week runtime. The offer amounts per bucket vary by campaign and goal. The Urgency Offer experiment used $100, $125, $150, and $200. Other VEGA-powered campaigns use entirely different amounts depending on what behavior is being incentivized. The framework stays consistent. The amounts flex.

4

Result: $125 Won Across Promo Testing. VEGA Then Expanded to Evergreen.

+18% lift in 30D DDers came from VEGA being applied across promo campaigns. Because promos performed well, VEGA was expanded to power all evergreen C1 referral communications. Rolled to 23.5M active C1 members. Approximately 20K newly enrolled C1 members targeted per day ongoing.

5

What's Next: DSML-Optimized Variable Baseline

Building on VEGA's randomized testing data, the team launched a new experiment this quarter. A T-learner uplift model now serves $100/$150/$200 offers to 15.4M C1 members based on predicted incremental conversions per dollar. These amounts reflect this specific baseline test and can change by campaign goal. It concentrates spend on members who genuinely respond to higher offers and avoids overpaying members who would refer at $100 anyway. This is the next ML model VEGA's data was training.

What I Learned and How I'd Apply It

From Chime's referral lifecycle to Wealthfront's lending expansion.

Key Learnings

Spend where it actually changes behavior. The first DSML model gave the highest incentives to members most likely to refer anyway, wasting budget on people who would have converted at $100. VEGA's randomized testing across promo campaigns collected the behavioral data to fix that. The +18% lift across promos proved the framework worked, which justified expanding it to evergreen. At Wealthfront this same principle applies to lending. We don't know yet which message, offer, or entry point converts a member into starting a loan application. The answer is in the data. The way to get the data is to randomize first, then let the model optimize.

The analyst's list and Braze's list are never the same list. During the VEGA rollout, Engineering bucketed 7M members but Braze delivered to significantly fewer because of unsubscribes, disabled push, and deliverability filters. The 24-hour check caught that gap, triggered backfills, and identified in-app and inbox as alternate channels for members who could no longer be reached by email or push.

Day 8 works because referred members need more runway than organic ones. The experiment data showed Day 0 had lower CVR because referred members hadn't had enough time to understand Chime yet. They were still in the signing-up-for-the-incentive mindset. Day 18 was too late. The consideration window had closed and engagement had dropped off. Day 8 hit the exact moment when the member had enough context to act but had not yet gone cold.

How I'd Think About Wealthfront Lending
01

Pre-Lending: Home Savings Journey

Who: High-value members in Texas, Colorado, or California with multiple product attaches and consistent account activity. Not pushing lending yet — identifying members who are financially stable enough to start thinking about homeownership.
What: A behavior-triggered canvas that guides members to open a dedicated home savings account separate from their existing Cash Account and Investing account. The goal is to build the down payment before they ever apply for a mortgage.
How: Series of milestone-triggered comms — not a one-time blast. First message introduces the idea of separating home savings from other investments. Each subsequent message triggers based on how much the member has deposited into the dedicated account. Member hits a savings checkpoint, they get a congratulatory message and guidance on the next step. Final trigger: member reaches a threshold amount AND their other accounts remain healthy and untouched — that combination signals readiness for a lending conversation. As members hit savings milestones, dynamically populate their estimated lending eligibility amount in the milestone comms based on their current Wealthfront financial profile. A member with a high investing balance might see "based on your profile you could qualify for up to $500K." A member earlier in their savings journey might see "based on your profile you could qualify for up to $200K." The personalized amount makes the home feel achievable and specific to them.
Why: Most lifecycle programs wait for a member to show explicit intent like visiting the lending page. This canvas builds intent by guiding members toward readiness. By the time they are ready to apply they already have a relationship with Wealthfront's home journey and money set aside for a down payment.
Measuring: Account open rate for dedicated home savings account, deposit frequency, milestone completion rate, and handoff rate from savings canvas to lending canvas.

02

Intent Signal: Lending Page Browse

Who: Existing members in Texas, Colorado, or California who have browsed the lending page but have not started an application.
What: Behavior-triggered re-engagement canvas that meets the member at the moment of expressed intent and removes friction from the application process.
How: Apply the same randomized testing methodology used to build VEGA but instead of populating a referral incentive, dynamically populate each member's personalized estimated loan eligibility amount based on their Wealthfront financial profile. Members with high account balances and multiple product attaches see a higher personalized amount. Members with lower balances see a figure that connects to where they are in their savings journey. Test which personalized amount threshold drives the most application starts before optimizing.
Why: Browsing the lending page is the strongest intent signal available without asking the member directly. The mistake would be sending a generic lending pitch to everyone. VEGA proved that spending where it actually changes behavior requires knowing which message moves which member.
Measuring: Lending application start rate by message variant vs. holdout. Application completion rate.

03

Expansion State Warm Leads

Who: Existing Wealthfront members in states where lending just launched beyond the initial three.
What: Personalized lending awareness campaign targeting members we can already reach through known channels.
How: Geo-trigger on state eligibility. Before building the audience confirm reachable channels — the analyst's list and Braze's list are never the same list. Validate deliverable audience size before launch. Personalize by product: Cash Account members get a savings-to-homeownership narrative, Investing members get a wealth-building plus homeownership narrative. Do not send a generic lending announcement to everyone in the state.
Why: Existing members in new states are the warmest possible cold audience. We already know their product usage, their balance behavior, and how to reach them. No list gap surprise if we validate before launch.
Measuring: Lending page CTR and application start rate among existing members vs. cold audience benchmark.

04

Randomize First. Optimize Second.

Who: All three audiences above once baseline behavioral data exists.
What: Apply VEGA-style variable testing to lending conversion — messaging variants, entry timing, and savings milestone thresholds tested across randomized audience buckets before any optimization.
How: Randomize messaging variants and personalized loan eligibility amounts across equal audience buckets. Test whether showing a higher estimated eligibility amount drives more application starts than a lower one. Collect clean behavioral data on which amount and which message combination actually moves a member from browsing to applying. This is the same methodology used to build VEGA at Chime — randomize first, let the data build the model.
Why: Nobody at Wealthfront knows yet which message, which offer, or which moment converts a member into starting a loan application. The answer is in the data. The way to get the data is to randomize first, then optimize.
Measuring: Application start rate and completion rate by variant vs. holdout. Use results to inform a predictive model for lending conversion the same way VEGA's data trained the T-learner model.

How I Know It's Actually Working

From pre-launch validation to holdout-proven incrementality.

Pre-Launch
Step 1

Sizing with Analyst. Confirm eligible audience is large enough to reach stat sig. Example: VEGA sized at 7M+ members across 4 variants.

→
Step 2

Cohort Build with Engineering. Segment and Hightouch fire events that trigger canvas entry in Braze. Engineering confirms cohort builds are correct before anything launches.

→
Step 3

Holdout Established. 10% withheld before first send. Without this baseline no conversion number can be trusted.

During Launch
24hr

Entries vs cohort size match? Messages sending? Canvas firing correctly across all touchpoints? Confirm canvas entries in Braze match the cohort size Engineering bucketed. If numbers don't align the experiment is paused before it runs.

3D

Open rates, click rates, delivery rate. Are members progressing through each canvas step? Example: VEGA-powered Urgency Offer open rate tracking above benchmark.

7D

Exits and conversions starting to show. Which touchpoints are driving action? Example: Mystery Bonus Day 8 group showing stronger DD intent than Day 0.

30D

Full measurement window. CRM data cross-referenced with analyst backend data. Enrollments, referral send rate, card activation, direct deposit CVR, holdout comparison. Example: VEGA across promo campaigns showed +18% lift vs holdout, which justified expanding the framework to evergreen communications.

Post-Launch
CRM Platform

Engagement signal only. Opens, clicks, sends, canvas entries, message volume per touchpoint. Never used alone for budget decisions.

Internal Validation

Analyst cross-references CRM data against backend conversion data using 24hr click window. Removes false positives.

Holdout-Validated Incremental

The only number used for budget decisions. What did we actually cause vs what would have happened organically. This is how VEGA's +18% and Mystery Bonus Day 8 win were confirmed. For GA evergreen canvases a permanent 5% universal control group is maintained to measure ongoing incremental impact of always-on programs.
