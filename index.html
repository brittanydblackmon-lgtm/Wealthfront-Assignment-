VEGA: Variable Evergreen General Audience

From failed ML model to personalized offer engine scaling to 23.5M members

1

The Hypothesis

Members convert better when the offer reflects their predicted likelihood, not a flat $100 for everyone.

2

First Attempt: DSML Optimization

ML-powered targeting yielded neutral lift after adjusting for sample imbalance. Diagnosed the failure and pivoted fast.

3

The Pivot: Randomized Offer Testing

Shifted to randomized offer amounts ($100, $125, $150, $200) across 7M+ members. Proper holdout via ExPlat. 50/50 split. 11-week runtime.

4

Result: $125 Variant Won. Scaled to GA.

+18% lift in 30D DDers. All variants positive ROI. Rolled to 23.5M active members. ~20K newly enrolled members targeted per day ongoing.

5

What's Next: DSML-Optimized Variable Baseline

Building on VEGA's randomized testing data, the team launched a new experiment on May 20, 2026. A T-learner uplift model now serves $100/$150/$200 offers to 15.4M C1 members based on predicted incremental conversions per dollar. It concentrates spend on members who genuinely respond to higher offers and avoids overpaying members who would refer at $100 anyway. This is the next ML model VEGA's data was training.

What I Learned and How I'd Apply It

From Chime's referral and funding lifecycle to Wealthfront's lending expansion

Key Learnings

Personalization beats volume. VEGA proved the right offer to the right person outperforms blasting everyone, even when the ML model fails on the first pass.

Measure what you cause. Holdout groups are non-negotiable. Incremental lift is the only number that matters for budget decisions.

Multichannel means meeting people where they are. Apple Pay's success came from 5 channels each doing a different job in the journey.

How I'd Think About Wealthfront Lending
01

Canvas 01

Who: Existing members using Cash Account or Investing.
What: Cross-sell lending as the next product in their journey.
How: Trigger on lending page browse or homeownership goal in Path, then personalize to their actual financial profile.
Why: Warmest audience because they already trust Wealthfront with their money.
Measuring: Lending page CTR and loan application start rate.

02

Canvas 02

Who: Members who signed up but have not activated across products.
What: Use lending as the entry point into the full ecosystem.
How: A/B test Cash Account plus Lending vs. Investing plus Lending as the intro offer with holdout groups using the same methodology as VEGA.
Why: Lending can pull low-engagement members deeper into the platform.
Measuring: Multi-product activation rate vs. holdout.

03

Canvas 03

Who: Existing Wealthfront members in newly launched lending states.
What: Personalized lending offer based on current product usage.
How: Geo-trigger on state eligibility and personalize by product, where Cash Account members get a savings-to-homeownership narrative and Investing members get a wealth-building narrative.
Why: Existing members in new states are warm leads and far cheaper to convert than cold audiences.
Measuring: Lending application start rate vs. cold audience benchmark.

The through line from Chime to Wealthfront: bring personalization + experimentation discipline to lending so each canvas is behavior-triggered, holdout-measured, and scaled only when incremental lift is proven.

Three Numbers. Only One That Matters for Budget Decisions.

How I know lifecycle programs are truly working: platform signal, internal validation, then causal incrementality.

X: Platform Reported

What the ESP reports. Always overstates impact because it includes organic conversions that would have happened anyway. Use this for deliverability and engagement monitoring only, never for budget decisions.

Y: Internally Validated

Platform data cross-referenced with internal data warehouse using a 24-hour click window. Removes false positives from long attribution windows. More accurate but still not the source of truth.

Z: Holdout-Validated Incremental

The only number used for budget decisions. Requires a global holdout group withheld from all sends before the first message goes out. Measures what we actually caused vs. what would have happened organically. This is how VEGA's +18% lift was validated.

Experiment Design Principles: Why Each Number Matters
10%
Holdout: without this baseline you cannot separate lifecycle-driven conversions from organic behavior
95%
Confidence: below this threshold any lift could be noise
30D
Runtime: considered purchases need full measurement windows or you undercount late converters
1
Conversion per member: prevents double-counting members who convert multiple times
